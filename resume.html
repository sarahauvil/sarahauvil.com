I graduated magna cum laude with a bachelor of science degree in digital information design.

I graduated from Winthrop University in Rock Hill, SC in May 2014. I completed a concentration in digital mass media and a minor in international studies with a focus in Asia.

Web designing since I taught myself HTML & CSS at age 11.

I learned how to code and design layouts and make icon gifs through the online journal community. I wanted to make custom themes, so I dove into coding. Eventually that passion led me to pursue web design as a career. In college, I began to hone my skills in programming, design and writing. I worked as a freelance writer and photographer for the Rock Hill Herald and a section editor of Winthrop's student newspaper, The Johnsonian, managing a team of 4-6 writers on tight deadlines.

Becoming an interface copy stickler and obsessed with content strategy.

During that time, I began honing my skills in writing web copy. I landed an internship at Ephricon Web Marketing and learned the ropes of SEO, PPC and affiliate marketing. That writing background never left, as I often prefer to write my own interface copy if there's no copywriter available. Designing an effective user flow without considering the implications of what the user is being told is impossible.

Meeting a user experience professional changed everything.

As a college junior, I had an unusual instructor who happened to be a usability engineer and interaction designer with over a decade of UX experience. In her class, I learned how to create complex visual data layouts, design app prototypes and wireframes, test my designs (which hey, was kinda fun! and I was really good at it). I originally got into web design because I wanted to make the online community I was part of a better place - and in UX, the heart of everything is in the people behind the screens. I enjoyed learning about this secret world of prototypes, research labs and cutting-edge web design.

Launching headfirst into UX design in finance and utilities.

My first full time position as a UX designer at a FINRA company in the heavily regulated financial sector. Within months, I went from taking school exams to designing responsive binomial probability calculators and transfer fund widgets for a real time streaming platform, TradeKing LIVE. I worked on the information architecture, stakeholder presentations, complex responsive design and navigation systems, sign up forms and user flows. I also worked to refine the UX funnel during the initial launch for TradeKing Advisors, testing designs many times for quality assurance.

I now work at Duke Energy alongside an awesome team of other user experience designers and front end developers. Some areas I'm heavily involved in include responsive prototyping, wireframing, UI design, usability testing and accessible design implementation.
